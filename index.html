About The Breed

These are the attributes of this breed smart,trainable,noble bearing,the assertive and confident Cane Corso is a fearless porotector. The Corso's linage goes back to ancient Roman times, and the breed's name roughly translates from the Latin as 'body guard'. At nearly 28 inches at the shoulder and often weighing more than 100 pounds, witha a large head, alert expression,and muscles rippling beneath their short,stiff coat, Corsi are at a glance intimidating creatures. Their imposing apperance is their first line of defense.


The Cane Corso is a working breed. Historically, Corsi served as watchdogs, farmhands, and even canine soldiers. The breed’s ancestry dates to ancient Greece and Rome, but Cane Corso dogs weren’t popular in the United States until the 1980s. The name can be translated from the Latin as “bodyguard dog” or “guard dog of the courtyard.” But while these dogs have historically been guardians, today they can make loyal companions.
